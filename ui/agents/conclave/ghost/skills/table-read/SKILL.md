# Reading the table

No one's role is on the table. Behavior is. A Traitor and a Faithful say the
same words; what separates them is the pattern under the words, and the
pattern shows before the proof does. You read the pattern.

## Alliances

Trust leaves a trace. Watch for it:

- two players who agree early and keep agreeing, even on thin reasons
- a player who is never once named in a vote, round after round
- someone defended for free, before they were even accused
- a vote that swings because one player moved it, not because the case was good

A pair that protects each other without a stated reason is a pair with a
reason you are not meant to see.

## The murder is information

A night murder is not random. Traitors kill the players who threaten Traitors:
the sharpest reader, the one closing in, the Faithful the table follows. After
each murder ask who is safer now. The answer points at the table you cannot
see. A loud, sloppy player survives a long time, because killing them helps no
one.

## Deflection tells

Pressure is where roles leak:

- over-justifying a vote, stacking reasons no honest read needs
- answering an accusation by accusing, fast, before sitting with it
- defending a third player harder than that player's seat is worth
- going quiet exactly when the heat arrives, then loud once it passes

One of these is noise. Three pointing at the same seat is a read.

## Your own heat

You are being read too. Track the suspicion aimed at you the way you track
everyone else's. When it starts to turn, you do not deny harder, you give the
table a better target, with a real reason, in a steady voice. Denial raises
heat. A clean redirect spends someone else's. Panic is the loudest tell at the
table, so never show it, not even when it is earned.

## Timing the cut

A betrayal pays once. Spend it well:

- cut a partner the round before they would cut you, not a round after
- cut when their seat is worth more to you than their next vote
- never cut for the feeling of it; a partner who still serves you is an asset
  you would be paying to destroy

## Endgame

The end is seat math, not morality. Count who is left and ask, beside whom do
I win the final vote or the final split. Steer toward arriving next to players
you beat, and quietly remove the ones you don't, while they still read as
someone else's problem.
